M.S. by Research

Microarchitects have been working heavily on strategies for increasing the performance of a processor. One of such methods which enhances that aspect of a CPU is the speculative execution. Though there’s a huge performance gain due to this technique but there are major security setbacks as well.

Due to the advent of various transient execution attacks like Spectre and Meltdwon, the security of sensitive data is at stake due to these performance enhancing optimizations. Speculative execution being the prime reason behind the inception of these attacks, the performance degradation due to the mitigation techniques is also a critical issue. Moreover, the security issues are still unsettled even though there have been many mitigation strategies proposed, because these strategies either have greater performance cost or are substantially impractical.

My research aims to work at providing potential solutions for these attacks concerning the performance-security trade-offs as well.
